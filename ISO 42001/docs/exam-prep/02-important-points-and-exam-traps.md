# Important Points and Exam Traps

## High-Value Points to Remember

* ISO 42001 is a management system standard for AI, not only a technical control list.
* PDCA is the simplest way to explain clause logic in an exam answer.
* Context, risk assessment, and objectives form a foundation for the entire AIMS.
* Top management owns the policy and management review.
* AI policy should provide a framework for setting objectives.
* Statement of applicability belongs to planning and control selection.
* AI risk assessment and AI system impact assessment are distinct.
* Monitoring results, audit outputs, and management review outputs are all evidence.
* Continual improvement is wider than corrective action.

## Common Exam Traps

* Treating scope as the first step without first understanding context.
* Saying the policy is written by top management personally. Accountability is theirs, drafting may be delegated.
* Confusing interested parties with all possible stakeholders instead of relevant interested parties.
* Assuming risk assessment is only asset-based.
* Saying objectives can be high level without measurements.
* Treating statement of applicability as an operational document instead of a planning baseline.
* Merging AI risk assessment with AI system impact assessment.
* Saying management review can be performed by department managers instead of top management.
* Confusing correction with corrective action.
* Presenting continual improvement as only the result of nonconformities.

## Auditor Mindset Reminders

The trainer repeatedly used an auditor perspective. In exam questions, think:

* What evidence would prove this requirement?
* Who is accountable for it?
* How is it linked to earlier clauses?
* Can the organization demonstrate traceability from context to risk to action?

## Evidence Examples to Mention in Answers

* Context and interested party records
* Scope statement
* Approved AI policy
* Roles and responsibilities matrix
* Risk assessment process and results
* Statement of applicability
* AI system impact assessment records
* Objective and KPI tracking
* Controlled documented information
* Monitoring and measurement evidence
* Internal audit program and audit reports
* Management review minutes or outputs
* Nonconformity and corrective action records

## Short Memory Hooks

* Clause 4 asks what environment the AIMS operates in.
* Clause 5 asks who leads and commits.
* Clause 6 asks what is planned and why.
* Clause 7 asks what support exists.
* Clause 8 asks what is actually done.
* Clause 9 asks how performance is checked.
* Clause 10 asks how the system improves.
