# Activity to Exam Mapping

## Why the Activities Matter

The training activities are not separate from the exam material. They are practical ways of testing whether you understand the logic of implementation and audit evidence.

## Activity Themes Seen in Day 1 to Day 3

### Identifying AI Systems

What it teaches:

* You cannot govern what you have not identified.
* Inventory and scope are linked.

Likely exam angle:

* Early implementation steps
* Scope and system boundary questions

### Context and Interested Parties

What it teaches:

* Relevant internal and external issues matter.
* Interested party needs and expectations drive requirements.

Likely exam angle:

* Clause 4 interpretation
* Traceability into planning and risk

### Policy and Objectives

What it teaches:

* Policy gives direction.
* Objectives must be aligned and measurable.

Likely exam angle:

* Leadership responsibility
* Objective setting and measurement

### Risk Assessment and Impact Assessment

What it teaches:

* Analysis must be structured and documented.
* Impact and risk are related but different.

Likely exam angle:

* Distinguishing terms correctly
* Showing implementation sequence

### Flashcard and True or False Activities

What it teaches:

* Small wording changes can make a statement false.
* The exam may test precise interpretation of clause language.

Likely exam angle:

* Spotting false or misleading statements
* Identifying who is responsible and what must be documented

### Nonconformity and Corrective Action Activity

What it teaches:

* A response is not automatically acceptable.
* Root cause and effectiveness review matter.

Likely exam angle:

* Difference between correction and corrective action
* Evaluating adequacy of response

### Implementation Flow and Gantt Thinking

What it teaches:

* Activities are linked and should be sequenced realistically.
* Some outputs become inputs to later work.

Likely exam angle:

* Order of implementation
* Dependency between clauses and outputs

## Best Way to Revise from the Activities

* Ask what output the activity produces.
* Ask which clause that output supports.
* Ask what evidence an auditor would expect.
* Ask what mistake would make the activity result unacceptable.
