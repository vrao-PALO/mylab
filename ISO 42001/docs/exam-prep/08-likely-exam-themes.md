# Likely Exam Themes

This sheet prioritizes the themes most likely to matter in a Lead Implementer style exam based on your Day 1 to Day 3 transcripts, the clause structure of ISO/IEC 42001, and public 2026 exam-prep trends.

## Tier 1: Highest Priority

### 1. PDCA and Clause 4 to 10 Logic

Why it is likely:

* It is the backbone of the whole standard.
* Trainers repeatedly explain the standard through PDCA.
* Many scenario answers become easier if you map them to Plan, Do, Check, Act.

What to know:

* Clause flow and dependency
* Why context comes before scope
* Why planning outputs feed operation
* Why performance evaluation feeds improvement

### 2. Context, Interested Parties, and Scope

Why it is likely:

* The transcripts repeatedly return to context as the foundation.
* Public prep trends still emphasize Clause 4 as a major differentiator.

What to know:

* Internal and external issues
* Relevant interested parties and their requirements
* How those requirements become management system inputs
* Why scope must reflect actual context and AI role

Typical trap:

* Writing generic stakeholder lists without relevance or traceability.

### 3. Leadership, Policy, and Accountability

Why it is likely:

* Leadership is central in both transcripts and provider training pages.
* Exams often test responsibility and accountability wording.

What to know:

* Top management responsibilities
* Policy approval and leadership commitment
* Role and responsibility assignment
* Policy as framework for objectives

Typical trap:

* Saying top management must personally draft the policy.

### 4. AI Risk Assessment vs AI System Impact Assessment

Why it is likely:

* It is one of the strongest repeated distinctions in the training.
* 2026 research shows growing importance of impact assessment through ISO/IEC 42005:2025.

What to know:

* Risk assessment purpose
* Impact assessment purpose
* Differences between organizational risk and broader consequences
* Why both are needed

Typical trap:

* Merging both into one answer or treating impact assessment as optional.

### 5. Objectives, Measurement, and Monitoring

Why it is likely:

* The transcripts repeatedly stress that objectives must be measurable.
* Monitoring and evidence are major audit themes.

What to know:

* Objectives must align to policy
* Objectives come after analysis
* KPI or measurement expectation
* Monitoring method, timing, responsibility, and evaluation

Typical trap:

* Giving broad objectives with no measurable basis.

## Tier 2: Very Likely

### 6. Statement of Applicability and Annex A Logic

Why it is likely:

* It is a classic management-system exam topic.
* Public 2026 material increasingly highlights Annex A controls and the SoA.

What to know:

* SoA as control baseline
* Selection and exclusion rationale
* Link to risk treatment
* Why it belongs to planning

Typical trap:

* Treating the SoA as an operational record rather than a planning baseline.

### 7. Documented Information and Evidence

Why it is likely:

* Real-world implementation trends show evidence collection is a bottleneck.
* Auditors and trainers both focus on proof, not just paperwork.

What to know:

* What documented information means
* Current and approved versions
* Retained records as evidence
* Evidence examples across clauses

Typical trap:

* Assuming a policy alone is enough proof of implementation.

### 8. Internal Audit and Management Review

Why it is likely:

* Clause 9 is a standard exam favorite across ISO schemes.
* The transcripts repeatedly use the auditor lens.

What to know:

* Audit objectivity and impartiality
* Audit scope and criteria
* Management review by top management
* Suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness
* Documented outputs from management review

Typical trap:

* Confusing management review with an operational meeting.

### 9. Nonconformity, Correction, Corrective Action, Continual Improvement

Why it is likely:

* The transcripts include detailed discussion and exercises on this topic.
* It is a reliable exam area because candidates often mix the terms.

What to know:

* Difference between correction and corrective action
* Need for cause analysis
* Continual improvement as broader than reacting to failures

Typical trap:

* Jumping from finding to action without root cause thinking.

## Tier 3: Emerging 2026 Themes

### 10. AI Inventory, Shadow AI, and AI Ecosystem Roles

Why it is likely:

* Public implementation guidance increasingly highlights AI inventory problems.
* Scope and AI role definition are becoming more practical and realistic topics.

What to know:

* Why AI inventory matters
* Shadow AI risk
* Provider, customer, partner, or related roles
* Scoping strategy

### 11. Regulatory Alignment Without Overclaiming Compliance

Why it is likely:

* The market is connecting ISO 42001 with AI regulation readiness.
* The EU AI Act is a strong current influence in 2026 discourse.

What to know:

* ISO 42001 supports governance and readiness
* It is not automatic proof of legal compliance
* Alignment is helpful, not equivalent

Typical trap:

* Saying certification alone proves full regulatory conformity.

### 12. Certification Ecosystem Maturity

Why it is likely:

* ISO/IEC 42006:2025 is now relevant to how certification bodies operate.
* This signals a more mature assurance environment around ISO 42001.

What to know:

* Certification quality depends on competent and credible auditors and bodies
* This theme is more useful for awareness and advanced answers than for basic clause recall

## How to Use This Sheet

If you have limited time, revise in this order:

1. PDCA and clause flow
2. Context, scope, policy, leadership
3. Risk assessment vs impact assessment
4. Objectives, monitoring, evidence
5. SoA, audit, management review, corrective action
6. 2026 themes such as AI inventory and regulatory alignment

## Best Last-Minute Focus Questions

* Can I explain Clause 4 to 10 without notes?
* Can I clearly distinguish risk assessment from impact assessment?
* Can I explain who owns policy, review, and accountability?
* Can I describe what evidence would prove conformity?
* Can I explain why SoA, monitoring, and corrective action matter?
