# Clause 4 to 10 Study Notes

## Core Logic

ISO 42001 should be understood as a management system, not only as a list of AI controls.
The trainer repeatedly emphasized PDCA:

* Plan: Clauses 4, 5, 6, and much of 7
* Do: Clause 8
* Check: Clause 9
* Act: Clause 10

A recurring message in the training was that clause flow matters. Context drives scope. Scope and context inform policy. Policy supports objectives. Risk and impact work shape controls, operations, monitoring, and improvement.

## Clause 4: Context of the Organization

Key ideas:

* Understand internal and external issues relevant to the AIMS.
* Identify relevant interested parties and determine their requirements.
* Define the scope of the AIMS based on that context.
* Establish the management system at a high level first and refine it iteratively.

Exam focus:

* Context is not an academic exercise.
* Interested party requirements become management system requirements when relevant.
* Scope should not be defined in isolation.
* Traceability between context and later risk assessment is important.

## Clause 5: Leadership

Key ideas:

* Top management is responsible for establishing the AI policy.
* Leadership must demonstrate commitment, not just delegate paperwork.
* Roles, responsibilities, and authorities need to be assigned and understood.
* The policy should be suitable for the organization and provide a framework for objectives.

Exam focus:

* Top management is accountable even if someone else drafts the document.
* The policy is not only a statement of intent; it must drive direction.
* Leadership evidence may appear in policy approval, resource decisions, reviews, and support for responsible roles.

## Clause 6: Planning

Key ideas:

* Planning includes actions to address risks and opportunities.
* AI risk assessment is central and must be documented as a process.
* AI objectives should be consistent with the policy and measurable.
* Statement of applicability is produced in planning, not in operations.

Exam focus:

* The training stressed the link between context and risk assessment.
* AIMS risk thinking is strongly context-based, not only asset-based.
* Objectives come after risk and impact thinking, not before.
* An objective without measurement is weak and exam-relevant as an error.

## AI Risk Assessment vs AI System Impact Assessment

This was one of the most important distinctions in the training.

AI risk assessment:

* Focuses on management system risks and AI-related risks that may affect intended outcomes.
* Supports treatment decisions and planning.

AI system impact assessment:

* Focuses on potential technical and societal consequences of a specific AI system.
* Must consider context, affected people, and consequences.
* Results should be documented and may be shared with relevant interested parties where appropriate.

Exam focus:

* They are related but not the same.
* Do not merge them into one concept in an answer.

## Clause 7: Support

Key ideas:

* Resources, competence, awareness, communication, and documented information support the system.
* Documented information should exist to the extent necessary.
* People need to know the policy, their role, and the implications of not conforming.

Exam focus:

* Clause 7 is not just documents.
* Communication includes what to communicate, when, to whom, and by whom.
* Document control means approved, current, available, and maintained evidence.

## Clause 8: Operation

Key ideas:

* Execute what was planned.
* Operate processes under controlled conditions.
* Review consequences of planned changes.
* Apply risk assessment and impact assessment in operational reality.

Exam focus:

* Clause 8 is about execution, not just writing procedures.
* Planning and operation should line up. If clause 6 says a process exists, clause 8 should show it being used.
* The trainer emphasized that documented information should be available to the extent necessary for confidence and evidence.

## Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

Key ideas:

* Monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation come first.
* Internal audit checks whether the system conforms and is effectively implemented.
* Management review is done by top management at planned intervals.

Exam focus:

* Monitoring must define method, timing, responsibility, and evaluation approach.
* Internal audit requires objectivity and impartiality.
* Management review evaluates suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness.
* Results of management review must be documented.

## Clause 10: Improvement

Key ideas:

* Nonconformity and corrective action are part of improvement.
* Continual improvement is broader than reacting to failures.
* Root cause matters before corrective action is defined.

Exam focus:

* Correction is not the same as corrective action.
* A correction contains or fixes the immediate issue.
* Corrective action addresses the cause to prevent recurrence.
* Continual improvement should remain the overall goal.

## Practical Implementation Sequence

A useful study sequence from the training is:

* Identify AI systems.
* Understand context and interested parties.
* Define scope.
* Establish policy and roles.
* Perform risk assessment and impact assessment.
* Define objectives and treatment plans.
* Control support processes and documented information.
* Run operations.
* Monitor, audit, and review.
* Improve and repeat through PDCA.
