# ISO 42001 Flashcards

Use these for quick recall, self-testing, or later conversion into physical cards or Anki-style prompts.

## Core Concepts

**Q1. What is ISO/IEC 42001?**  
A. It is the international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System, or AIMS.

**Q2. What is the easiest way to remember the logic of ISO 42001?**  
A. Use PDCA: Plan, Do, Check, Act.

**Q3. Which clauses broadly fit into Plan?**  
A. Clauses 4, 5, 6, and much of 7.

**Q4. Which clause is mainly Do?**  
A. Clause 8.

**Q5. Which clause is mainly Check?**  
A. Clause 9.

**Q6. Which clause is mainly Act?**  
A. Clause 10.

**Q7. What is the main difference between AIMS and a simple control checklist?**  
A. AIMS is a management system with governance, objectives, processes, monitoring, review, and continual improvement.

## Clause 4: Context

**Q8. What does Clause 4 require first?**  
A. Understanding the organization’s internal and external issues relevant to the AIMS.

**Q9. What is the role of interested parties in Clause 4?**  
A. Relevant interested parties and their requirements help define management system requirements.

**Q10. Why should scope not be defined in isolation?**  
A. Because scope must reflect context, interested party requirements, and the organization’s actual AI activities.

**Q11. What is an exam trap related to interested parties?**  
A. Confusing all possible stakeholders with relevant interested parties.

**Q12. What implementation output usually follows context analysis?**  
A. Scope definition for the AIMS.

## Clause 5: Leadership

**Q13. Who is responsible for establishing the AI policy?**  
A. Top management.

**Q14. Does top management have to personally draft the AI policy?**  
A. No. They are accountable for establishing it, even if someone else drafts it.

**Q15. What must the AI policy provide besides direction?**  
A. A framework for setting AI objectives.

**Q16. What is one way leadership demonstrates commitment?**  
A. By approving policy, supporting objectives, assigning roles, providing resources, and participating in management review.

**Q17. Why are roles and responsibilities important in Clause 5?**  
A. Because accountability for governance, risk, monitoring, and improvement must be clear.

## Clause 6: Planning

**Q18. What is the core planning focus of Clause 6?**  
A. Risks, opportunities, objectives, treatment planning, and related documented outputs.

**Q19. What is a major training emphasis about AI risk assessment?**  
A. It should be linked to context and relevant interested party requirements.

**Q20. What is the difference between context-based risk assessment and asset-only thinking?**  
A. Context-based assessment looks beyond assets and considers business environment, obligations, stakeholders, and AI-specific consequences.

**Q21. When should AI objectives be set?**  
A. After risk and impact analysis, not before.

**Q22. What makes an AI objective weak in an exam answer?**  
A. If it has no KPI, metric, or defined way to measure achievement.

**Q23. What is the Statement of Applicability?**  
A. A baseline document showing which Annex A controls are selected or excluded and why.

**Q24. In which phase is the Statement of Applicability established?**  
A. In planning, not in operations.

## Risk Assessment vs Impact Assessment

**Q25. What does AI risk assessment focus on?**  
A. Risks that could affect intended outcomes of the management system or AI-related governance objectives.

**Q26. What does AI system impact assessment focus on?**  
A. The potential technical and societal consequences of a specific AI system.

**Q27. Why is this distinction important in the exam?**  
A. Because they are related but not the same, and confusing them is a common mistake.

**Q28. Which supporting standard is especially relevant to AI system impact assessment in 2026?**  
A. ISO/IEC 42005:2025.

## Clause 7: Support

**Q29. What areas are covered by Clause 7?**  
A. Resources, competence, awareness, communication, and documented information.

**Q30. What does “documented information” mean in practice?**  
A. Necessary controlled documents and records that are current, usable, approved, and retained as evidence where required.

**Q31. What should communication planning define?**  
A. What to communicate, when, to whom, by whom, and sometimes how.

**Q32. What is a common exam trap in Clause 7?**  
A. Treating it as only document control instead of a broader support function.

## Clause 8: Operation

**Q33. What is the main purpose of Clause 8?**  
A. To execute what was planned and operate processes under controlled conditions.

**Q34. What is an easy way to identify a Clause 8 problem?**  
A. Clause 6 says a process exists, but Clause 8 cannot show that it is actually being used.

**Q35. Why is change control important in operations?**  
A. Because planned changes and their consequences must be reviewed and controlled.

**Q36. What kind of evidence often matters in Clause 8?**  
A. Testing logs, validation records, human oversight records, change logs, and operational use evidence.

## Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

**Q37. What comes first in performance evaluation?**  
A. Monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation.

**Q38. What must monitoring define to be credible?**  
A. Method, timing, responsibility, and how results will be analyzed or evaluated.

**Q39. What is the purpose of internal audit?**  
A. To check whether the AIMS conforms to requirements and is effectively implemented and maintained.

**Q40. Why must internal audit be objective and impartial?**  
A. Because audit results must be trustworthy and not influenced by self-review bias.

**Q41. Who performs management review?**  
A. Top management.

**Q42. What does management review evaluate?**  
A. Suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the AIMS.

**Q43. What must be documented from management review?**  
A. The results, including decisions and actions.

## Clause 10: Improvement

**Q44. What is the difference between correction and corrective action?**  
A. Correction fixes or contains the immediate issue; corrective action addresses the underlying cause to prevent recurrence.

**Q45. What must happen before corrective action is well defined?**  
A. Cause analysis or root cause understanding.

**Q46. Why is continual improvement broader than corrective action?**  
A. Because improvement can also come from proactive enhancement, not only from failures or nonconformities.

## Annexes and Supporting Standards

**Q47. How many Annex A controls are commonly referenced in ISO 42001 public guidance?**  
A. 38 controls across nine domains.

**Q48. What does Annex B provide?**  
A. Implementation guidance for the Annex A controls.

**Q49. What does Annex C broadly help with?**  
A. It supports thinking about AI risk sources and objectives.

**Q50. What does ISO/IEC 42006:2025 add to the ecosystem?**  
A. Requirements for bodies that audit and certify AIMS according to ISO/IEC 42001.

## 2026 Trend Flashcards

**Q51. What is one of the strongest real-world implementation trends in 2026?**  
A. Building a complete AI inventory and addressing shadow AI.

**Q52. What is a major real-world bottleneck in ISO 42001 implementation?**  
A. Collecting and organizing operational evidence.

**Q53. Does ISO 42001 certification automatically equal EU AI Act compliance?**  
A. No. It supports readiness and overlap, but it is not automatic legal equivalence.

**Q54. What public-source trend suggests ISO 42001 is maturing quickly?**  
A. Enterprise certifications, supporting standards, and formal certification ecosystem development.

## Exam Strategy Flashcards

**Q55. If you forget an answer in the exam, what mindset should you use?**  
A. Think like an implementer and an auditor: who is responsible, what evidence exists, and how does it link to earlier clauses?

**Q56. What kind of answer usually scores better: abstract or traceable?**  
A. Traceable answers that connect context, risk, controls, evidence, and review.

**Q57. What is a safe memory hook for Clause 4 to 10?**  
A. Context, leadership, planning, support, operation, evaluation, improvement.

**Q58. What is a common weak answer style to avoid?**  
A. Listing generic AI ethics words without linking them to the management system requirements.

**Q59. What topic deserves extra attention based on both your transcripts and 2026 research?**  
A. AI system impact assessment.

**Q60. What are the best legitimate preparation sources?**  
A. Official ISO summaries, official provider information such as PECB, reputable implementation guides, and your own clause-based notes and practice questions.
