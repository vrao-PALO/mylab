# ISO 42001 Print-Friendly Q/A Booklet

This booklet converts the study pack into a cleaner question-and-answer format for reading, review, or printing.

## Section 1: Foundations

**1. What is ISO/IEC 42001?**

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System, or AIMS.

**2. What is the main purpose of ISO/IEC 42001?**

It provides a structured management-system approach for responsible AI governance, helping organizations manage AI-related risks and opportunities while supporting trust, transparency, and continual improvement.

**3. What is the simplest way to remember the structure of the standard?**

Use PDCA:

* Plan: Clauses 4, 5, 6, and much of 7
* Do: Clause 8
* Check: Clause 9
* Act: Clause 10

**4. Why is ISO 42001 more than a control checklist?**

Because it is a management system. It includes governance, leadership, objectives, planning, support, operations, monitoring, review, and improvement.

## Section 2: Clauses 4 to 10

**5. What does Clause 4 focus on?**

Clause 4 focuses on understanding the organization’s internal and external context, identifying relevant interested parties and their requirements, defining the scope, and establishing the AIMS.

**6. Why is context so important?**

Because context shapes scope, risk assessment, objectives, and later decisions. Without context, the management system becomes generic and less defensible.

**7. What is a common mistake with interested parties?**

A common mistake is listing every possible stakeholder instead of identifying relevant interested parties whose needs and expectations actually matter to the AIMS.

**8. What does Clause 5 require?**

Clause 5 requires top management leadership, commitment, policy establishment, and assignment of roles and responsibilities.

**9. Who is responsible for establishing the AI policy?**

Top management is responsible.

**10. Does top management need to personally write the policy?**

No. They are accountable for it, even if another person drafts it.

**11. What must the AI policy do?**

It must be appropriate to the organization and provide a framework for setting AI objectives.

**12. What does Clause 6 focus on?**

Clause 6 focuses on planning, including risks, opportunities, objectives, treatment planning, and related documented outputs.

**13. What is important about AI objectives?**

They must align to the AI policy and be measurable.

**14. When should objectives be defined?**

They should be defined after risk and impact thinking, not before.

**15. What does Clause 7 cover?**

Clause 7 covers support activities such as resources, competence, awareness, communication, and documented information.

**16. What does Clause 8 cover?**

Clause 8 covers operation, meaning execution of what was planned, controlled operation of processes, and handling of changes.

**17. What does Clause 9 cover?**

Clause 9 covers monitoring, measurement, analysis, evaluation, internal audit, and management review.

**18. What does Clause 10 cover?**

Clause 10 covers improvement, including nonconformity, corrective action, and continual improvement.

## Section 3: High-Probability Distinctions

**19. What is the difference between AI risk assessment and AI system impact assessment?**

AI risk assessment focuses on risks affecting the management system and AI governance outcomes. AI system impact assessment focuses on the technical and societal consequences of a specific AI system.

**20. Why is this distinction important?**

Because candidates often merge them into one concept, but the training clearly treats them as related yet separate.

**21. What is the difference between correction and corrective action?**

Correction is the immediate fix or containment. Corrective action addresses the underlying cause to prevent recurrence.

**22. Why is root cause important?**

Because corrective action is not sound unless the cause is understood first.

**23. What is the difference between policy and objectives?**

The policy sets direction and provides the framework. Objectives translate that direction into measurable outcomes.

## Section 4: Statement of Applicability and Annex A

**24. What is the Statement of Applicability?**

It is the baseline document that records which Annex A controls are selected or excluded and why.

**25. When is the Statement of Applicability established?**

It is established in planning, not in operations.

**26. Why is it important?**

It links risk treatment decisions to the selected control baseline and is one of the most important audit documents.

**27. What is commonly referenced about Annex A in public guidance?**

Public guidance commonly refers to 38 controls across nine domains.

## Section 5: Evidence and Audit Thinking

**28. What makes an answer stronger in a Lead Implementer exam?**

An answer is stronger when it shows traceability: context to risk, policy to objectives, planning to operation, monitoring to evidence, and review to improvement.

**29. What does documented information mean in practice?**

It means maintaining necessary controlled documents and records that are current, approved, accessible, and retained where evidence is required.

**30. What kinds of records often matter in ISO 42001 implementation?**

Examples include risk assessments, impact assessments, policies, objective tracking, testing logs, bias assessments, change records, internal audit outputs, management review results, and corrective action records.

**31. Why must internal audit be objective and impartial?**

Because audit results must be credible and not distorted by self-review.

**32. Who performs management review?**

Top management performs management review.

**33. What is management review supposed to evaluate?**

The suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the AIMS.

## Section 6: 2026 Ecosystem Updates

**34. What is one important supporting standard in 2026 for impact assessments?**

ISO/IEC 42005:2025.

**35. What is one important supporting standard in 2026 for certification bodies?**

ISO/IEC 42006:2025.

**36. What does the 2026 market signal about ISO 42001?**

It shows increasing maturity, enterprise adoption, and a more structured certification ecosystem.

**37. What real-world implementation themes are becoming more important?**

AI inventory, shadow AI, evidence collection, AI role definition, and regulatory alignment.

**38. Does ISO 42001 certification automatically mean EU AI Act compliance?**

No. It supports readiness and overlap, but it is not automatic legal equivalence.

## Section 7: Exam Traps

**39. What is a common mistake with scope?**

Defining scope before properly understanding context.

**40. What is a common mistake with objectives?**

Defining objectives without any KPI or measurement method.

**41. What is a common mistake with the SoA?**

Treating it as an operational record instead of a planning baseline.

**42. What is a common mistake with management review?**

Treating it as a department-level review instead of a top-management review.

**43. What is a common mistake with continual improvement?**

Thinking it only happens through nonconformity and corrective action.

## Section 8: Last-Minute Revision Questions

**44. Can you explain Clause 4 to 10 without notes?**

You should be able to explain the role of each clause and how each one connects to the next.

**45. Can you explain the difference between risk assessment and impact assessment clearly?**

You should be able to distinguish internal governance risk from broader technical and societal impact.

**46. Can you explain who owns policy, review, and accountability?**

You should remember that top management owns policy and management review, while roles and responsibilities distribute execution and oversight.

**47. Can you explain what evidence proves conformity?**

You should be able to cite records, logs, reports, approvals, audit outputs, and review results.

**48. What should you do if you are unsure in the exam?**

Answer from an implementer-and-auditor perspective: who is responsible, what evidence exists, and how does it link to the management-system flow?
