# ISO 42001 One-Page Cram Sheet

## 1. What ISO 42001 Is

* ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System, or AIMS.
* It is the management-system standard for responsible AI governance.
* Think management system, not only technical controls.

## 2. Fastest Memory Model

* Plan: Clauses 4, 5, 6, much of 7
* Do: Clause 8
* Check: Clause 9
* Act: Clause 10

## 3. Clause 4 to 10 in One Line Each

* Clause 4: Understand context, relevant interested parties, and scope.
* Clause 5: Top management leads, commits, sets policy, and assigns responsibilities.
* Clause 6: Plan for risks, opportunities, objectives, treatment, and SoA.
* Clause 7: Support with resources, competence, awareness, communication, and documented information.
* Clause 8: Run the planned processes and control operations and changes.
* Clause 9: Monitor, measure, audit, and review.
* Clause 10: Correct, fix causes, and improve continually.

## 4. Must-Remember Responsibilities

* Top management establishes the AI policy.
* Top management performs management review.
* Internal audit must be objective and impartial.
* Relevant interested parties matter, not every possible stakeholder.

## 5. High-Probability Exam Distinctions

### AI risk assessment vs AI system impact assessment

* Risk assessment: focuses on risks affecting AIMS outcomes and AI governance objectives.
* Impact assessment: focuses on technical and societal consequences of specific AI systems.
* They are related but not the same.

### Correction vs corrective action

* Correction: immediate fix or containment.
* Corrective action: action on the cause to prevent recurrence.

### Policy vs objectives

* Policy gives direction and framework.
* Objectives must align with policy and be measurable.

## 6. Statement of Applicability

* The SoA is a baseline document.
* It shows which Annex A controls are selected or excluded, and why.
* It belongs to planning, not operations.

## 7. What Makes Answers Strong

* Link context to risk.
* Link policy to objectives.
* Link planning to operation.
* Link monitoring to evidence.
* Link audit and review to improvement.

## 8. Common Exam Traps

* Defining scope before understanding context.
* Saying top management must personally write the policy.
* Confusing all stakeholders with relevant interested parties.
* Treating risk assessment as only asset-based.
* Giving objectives without KPIs or measurements.
* Treating SoA as an operational record.
* Merging risk assessment with impact assessment.
* Saying management review can be done only by department managers.
* Confusing correction with corrective action.
* Treating continual improvement as only nonconformity handling.

## 9. Evidence Words to Use in Answers

* Scope statement
* AI policy
* Roles and responsibilities matrix
* Risk assessment process and results
* Statement of applicability
* AI system impact assessment record
* Objective and KPI tracking
* Controlled documented information
* Monitoring and measurement evidence
* Internal audit program and reports
* Management review results
* Nonconformity and corrective action records

## 10. 2026 Update Points

* ISO/IEC 42005:2025 strengthens the impact-assessment side of the ecosystem.
* ISO/IEC 42006:2025 strengthens the certification-body and audit ecosystem.
* Enterprise adoption is increasing.
* AI inventory, shadow AI, and evidence collection are major real-world themes.
* ISO 42001 supports regulatory readiness, but it is not automatic EU AI Act compliance.

## 11. Last-Minute Exam Strategy

* Think like both an implementer and an auditor.
* Ask: who owns this, what evidence proves it, and how does it link to earlier clauses?
* If stuck, answer using PDCA and clause flow.
* If a statement looks too absolute, check whether the standard really says that.

## 12. Final Short Hooks

* Context before scope.
* Policy before objectives.
* Risk before treatment.
* Plan before operate.
* Evidence before assurance.
* Root cause before corrective action.
* Continual improvement is the bigger picture.
