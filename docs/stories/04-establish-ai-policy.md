# User Story: 4 - Establish AI Policy

**As a** top management representative,
**I want** to establish an AI policy,
**so that** the organization has a clear direction and framework for responsible AI objectives and controls.

## Acceptance Criteria

* The AI policy is approved by top management.
* The policy is appropriate to the organization s purpose and AI context.
* The policy provides a framework for setting AI objectives.
* The policy communicates commitments relevant to responsible AI, including fairness, transparency, and effectiveness.
* Personnel covered by the AIMS can access and understand the policy.

## Clause Reference

| Clause | Requirement |

## Key Findings from Training (Day 1 — Deep Dive)

* **Top management definition (ISO)**: "A person or group of people who direct and control the organization at the highest level." This is functional, not tied to title — could be CEO, MD, Board, or CXO depending on the organization.
* The trainer gave an explicit **non-compliance example**: assigning the AIMS manager four levels below the IT department head is a violation of this requirement — management system objectives must come from those who control strategic direction.
* **Policy is a framework, not a list of controls**: it sets the direction within which objectives are derived. It should reference fairness, effectiveness, and transparency explicitly.
* Policy must be **compatible with strategic direction**: the trainer asked, "If a patient data breach could close your healthcare facility, how important is data protection to top management?" — if they cannot articulate the strategic link, there is a disconnect the auditor will find.
* Drafting may be delegated to the AIMS lead; accountability and **signature cannot be delegated**.
* Policy must be available and communicated — not locked in a management system folder nobody reads.

## Risk Linkage (from 50 Sample AI Risks)

* Risk 44 — Lack of documented AI governance framework: policy is the foundational governance document
* Risk 39 — Lack of transparency in AI decision-making: policy must commit to transparency as a principle
* Risk 38 — Algorithmic discrimination: fairness commitment in policy drives downstream controls

## Implementation Notes

* Include the three AIMS objectives in the policy: Effectiveness (AI delivers intended outcomes), Fairness (AI does not discriminate against individuals or groups), Transparency (AI decisions are explainable).
* Link the policy explicitly to legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements applicable to AI.
* Review and update the policy as context, strategy, or regulations change.
* Use the policy review schedule (document template available: 1. AI Policy Review Schedule v1.0.pdf) to structure review cycles.
