# User Story: 12 - Conduct Internal Audit

**As a** internal auditor,
**I want** to conduct internal audits of the AIMS,
**so that** the organization can verify conformity and identify weaknesses before external audit or certification.

## Acceptance Criteria

* Internal audits are planned at defined intervals.
* Audit scope and criteria consider relevant risks and management system processes.
* Objective evidence is collected and retained.
* Audit findings are reported to relevant management.

## Key Findings from Training (Day 3)

* The trainer described auditing from the perspective of a third-party certification auditor at BSI Ś giving insight into what auditors truly look for versus what organizations often prepare.
* **Objectivity is non-negotiable**: An internal auditor cannot audit their own work. If the AIMS lead also performs the internal audit, that is a finding.
* The trainer shared an example of a European auditor who was pressured by a client with "if you give me a nonconformity, I will lose my job." The auditor reported it Ś both parties were penalized. The lesson: audit independence is a professional obligation.
* **Top management engagement during audit**: In a certification audit, top management is interviewed directly. If they cannot articulate how the management system links to business strategy, it is a finding against Clause 5.1.
* The trainer's view on nonconformities: organizations should be **grateful** for audit findings Ś "somebody has the guts to identify the gap." An audit with no findings is suspicious, not a success.
* Audit scope for AIMS should cover: context documentation, interested parties, policy, risk assessment, SoA, objectives, monitoring evidence, nonconformity records, and management review minutes.

## Implementation Notes

* Use template: 25. Internal Audit Schedule v1.0.pdf to structure the annual audit programme.
* Define audit criteria for each audit: which clauses, which controls, which processes will be evaluated.
* Collect evidence types: document review, interviews, system walkthroughs, sampling of records.
* Issue audit findings as: Conformity | Opportunity for Improvement | Nonconformity. Nonconformities require documented corrective action (Story 14).
* For small organizations: use an external party for at least partial audit coverage to ensure independence.
