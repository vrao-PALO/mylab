# User Story: 2 - Determine Context and Interested Parties

**As a** AIMS implementation lead,
**I want** to document the organization context and the requirements of relevant interested parties,
**so that** the AI management system reflects real business, regulatory, and stakeholder needs.

## Acceptance Criteria

* Internal and external issues relevant to the AI management system are documented.
* Relevant interested parties are identified.
* The requirements of those interested parties are captured and reviewed.
* The documented context is used as an input to planning and risk-related activities.

## Key Findings from Training (Day 2)

* The trainer was explicit: issues must be identified with respect to the three AIMS pillars Ś Effectiveness, Fairness, and Transparency Ś not all issues in general.
* **Internal issues framework**: SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses scoped to AIMS impact).
* **External issues framework**: PESTIL analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Information, Legal).
* Interested parties must pass a **relevance filter**: only those who affect or are affected by the AIMS.
* Critical distinction (trainer Day 2): In a B2B scenario (e.g., IT firm building mobile app for a bank), the most significant interested party for AIMS purposes is the **end customer of the bank** Ś the individual whose life is affected by the AI decision Ś not the B2B client paying for the service.
* The trainer used a credit score example: a wrong AI decision on a credit application impacts the applicant's loan, housing, education, and life Ś hence that person is the highest-priority interested party.
* Employees are always interested parties in AIMS (same principle as PIMS).

## Risk Linkage (from 50 Sample AI Risks)

* Risk 38 Ś Algorithmic discrimination: only surfaces if affected individuals are identified as interested parties
* Risk 39 Ś Lack of transparency in AI decision-making: regulatory interest = interested party requirement
* Risk 44 Ś Lack of documented AI governance: rooted in failure to understand context and stakeholder expectations
* Risk 46 Ś Non-compliance with privacy regulations: interested party requirement from regulators

## Implementation Notes

* Conduct SWOT for internal issues and PESTIL for external issues, scoped specifically to AI activities.
* Map each interested party to: Who they are | Why relevant | What they need | What they expect | How requirements become AIMS inputs.
* Do not confuse "needs" (minimum mandatory requirements) with "expectations" (desirable but not mandatory).
* Review and update context analysis at least annually, or whenever significant business or regulatory changes occur.
