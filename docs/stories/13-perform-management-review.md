# User Story: 13 - Perform Management Review

**As a** top management representative,
**I want** to conduct management reviews of the AIMS,
**so that** leadership can evaluate suitability, adequacy, effectiveness, and needed changes.

## Acceptance Criteria

* Management reviews are scheduled and conducted by top management.
* Review inputs include performance results, audit information, risks, and improvement needs.
* Review decisions and actions are recorded.
* Outputs are used to drive updates to the AIMS where needed.

## Clause Reference

| Clause | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| 9.3 | Top management shall review AIMS at planned intervals |
| 9.3.1 | Review considers: changes in external/internal issues, performance trends, risks, audit results, interested party feedback |
| 9.3.2 | Review outputs: decisions on AIMS suitability, adequacy, effectiveness; changes needed; resource decisions |
| 5.1 | Leadership demonstrated through participation in management review |

## Key Findings from Training (Days 1, 3 & 5)

* The trainer confirmed this on Day 3: a **department manager running a "management review" meeting** without top management involvement does not satisfy Clause 9.3. This is a commonly cited exam trap and a real-world audit finding.
* Management review is **not an operational status meeting** — it is a strategic review where top management evaluates whether the AIMS continues to fit the organization, is adequately resourced, and is achieving its intended results.
* The three evaluation criteria: (1) **Suitability** — does the AIMS still fit the organization's current context? (2) **Adequacy** — are the controls and resources sufficient? (3) **Effectiveness** — is the AIMS achieving its objectives?
* Review frequency: at minimum annually, but high-risk organizations or those in dynamic regulatory environments should review more frequently.
* Inputs must include: status of previous management review actions, changes in context, performance and measurement trends, audit findings, nonconformity and corrective action status, improvement opportunities.
* Outputs must be **documented** and actions tracked; they feed back into the AIMS planning cycle.

## Risk Linkage (from 50 Sample AI Risks)

* Risk 44 — Lack of documented AI governance framework: management review is the governance oversight mechanism at the top
* Risk 45 — Absence of AI risk management process: management review verifies the risk process is functioning
* Risk 13 — Model drift: management review should include performance trend data from AI systems
* Risk 22 — Lack of monitoring of model performance: monitoring data is a required input to management review

## Implementation Notes

* Use template: 24. Management Review Process v1.0.pdf to structure the agenda and outputs.
* Schedule management review into the leadership calendar at the start of each year — top management availability must be secured in advance to avoid deferral.
* Prepare a management review pack covering: objectives achievement status, risk posture update, audit findings summary, nonconformity trends, significant changes, resource considerations, improvement pipeline.
* Record decisions and assign owners for each action arising from the review.
