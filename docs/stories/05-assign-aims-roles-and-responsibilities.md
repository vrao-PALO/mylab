# User Story: 5 - Assign AIMS Roles and Responsibilities

**As a** top management representative,
**I want** to assign and communicate AIMS roles and responsibilities,
**so that** accountability for AI governance activities is clear across the organization.

## Acceptance Criteria

* Key AIMS roles are identified, including ownership for policy, risk, monitoring, and improvement activities.
* Responsibilities are assigned to named roles or positions.
* Role holders understand what they are accountable for.
* Leadership responsibilities and delegated responsibilities are both documented.

## Clause Reference

| Clause | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| 5.1 | Top management demonstrates leadership by supporting other relevant roles |
| 5.3 | Top management assigns roles, responsibilities and authorities |

## Key Findings from Training (Day 1)

* **Top management definition matters**: The trainer stressed that "top management" is not a title — it is the person or group who exercises control and direction at the highest level. Misidentifying this leads to governance gaps an auditor will find.
* **Non-compliance example cited**: An IT department assigned its AIMS manager four levels below the department head. The trainer identified this as non-compliant because a management system must receive its strategic direction from those who actually govern the organization.
* **Empowerment is a leadership act**: Clause 5.1 requires top management to support other relevant roles in demonstrating their leadership — meaning they must give people the authority, not just the title.
* The trainer distinguished **accountability** (stays with top management) from **responsibility** (can be delegated to AIMS lead, risk owner, process owner, etc.).
* Auditors in a top management interview will ask: "How does top management demonstrate leadership and commitment?" — the expected answers are: signing the policy, allocating resources, participating in management review, and communicating importance of AI management.

## Risk Linkage (from 50 Sample AI Risks)

* Risk 30 — Lack of defined AI system ownership: this story directly assigns system ownership
* Risk 44 — Lack of documented AI governance framework: clear roles are the structural backbone of governance
* Risk 25 — Inadequate incident response: requires a named role responsible for AI incidents

## Implementation Notes

* Document roles using the template available: 2. Roles and Responsibilities v1.0.pdf.
* Minimum roles to assign: AIMS Owner (top management), AIMS Lead (implementation), AI System Owner (per system), Risk Owner, Internal Auditor (requires independence from the function audited).
* Communicate role assignments through induction, training, and the communications plan (Story 10).
* Review role assignments when organizational structure changes.
