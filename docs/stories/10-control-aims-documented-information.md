# User Story: 10 - Control AIMS Documented Information

**As a** document control owner,
**I want** to control AIMS documented information,
**so that** policies, processes, templates, and records remain current, approved, and usable as evidence.

## Acceptance Criteria

* Required AIMS documents and records are identified.
* Documents have defined ownership, approval, and version status.
* Obsolete or changed documents are updated through a controlled process.
* Users can access the current approved versions needed for their work.

## Clause Reference

| Clause | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| 7.4 | Create and update documented information; control documented information |
| 7.4.1 | Documented information required by the standard and by the organization |
| 7.4.2 | Creation and updating: identification, format, media, review and approval |
| 7.4.3 | Control: availability, protection, distribution, access, retention, disposition |

## Key Findings from Training (Days 1, 3 & 5)

* The standard requires documented information "to the extent necessary to have confidence in effective implementation." This means documentation requirements are risk-proportionate — not a blanket requirement for every activity to be documented exhaustively.
* Two types of documented information under ISO 42001: **documents** (instructions, policies, procedures — kept current) and **records** (evidence of activities performed — retained, not modified).
* The trainer emphasized that documentation must be **controlled** — an undated, unsigned policy in a shared folder with multiple versions is an audit finding.
* Day 5 clarified that during the exam, annotating the MIMEOI copy of the standard is permitted because it does not modify the original — this reflects the same principle: controlled originals vs. personal working copies.
* **Communications plan** is part of this story: what information is communicated, when, to whom, how, and by whom — reference template: 20. Communications Plan and Procedure v1.0.pdf.

## Risk Linkage (from 50 Sample AI Risks)

* Risk 24 — Poor documentation of AI lifecycle processes: this story directly addresses it
* Risk 48 — Failure to maintain AI documentation and records: evidence retention is an acceptance criterion here
* Risk 28 — Insufficient change management procedures: change must be captured via controlled document update

## Implementation Notes

* Maintain a document register with: Document ID | Title | Version | Owner | Approver | Last Reviewed | Next Review | Status.
* Separate the register for documents (versioned, reviewed) from records (immutable evidence logs).
* Apply access controls: who can read, who can edit, who can approve — especially for policy and risk documents.
* Define retention periods for records — link to legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements.
* Leverage existing ISMS document management infrastructure where possible to reduce overhead.
